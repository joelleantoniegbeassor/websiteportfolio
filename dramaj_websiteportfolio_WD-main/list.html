001 Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley
(1) Edited excerpt from the original text: "Listen to my tale;
when you have heard that, abandon or commiserate me, as you shall
judge that I deserve. But hear me" (Shelley, 2003, p. 76).

In the original novel, the scientist Frankenstein creates a monster,
which is rejected and feared by all of humanity, including Frankenstein,
its creator. This leads to the monster begging Frankenstein
to create a second monster to save him from loneliness.

(2) This extract from the original novel (Shelley, 1987, p. 135) describes the
violence that the monster has repeatedly experienced from human society.

012 My Words to Victor Frankenstein Susan Stryker
«My words to Victor Frankenstein above the Village of Chamounix» is an
essay by Susan Stryker, a poetic yet scientific attempt to identify with the
monster's construction and becoming as a trans woman. The text serves
as a beautiful but potent threat to all those, who haven't made peace with
their inherent monstrosity.
